# Milkcrate Logo — Design Brief

## Summary

A simple geometric symbol mark (Cloudflare-like) for Milkcrate — warm, human, and abstract, suggesting discovery without literal crate or record imagery. Scales to favicon size, works in single color.

---

## Core Concept: The Open Crate

A flat horizontal base line (suggesting the crate floor). From it, a smooth arc rises diagonally — like a crate lid opening upward, or a record being lifted out. A short vertical line intersects the arc near its apex. At the intersection, a small triangular or diamond-shaped **negative-space window** opens up — the discovery point, the thing revealed.

**Shape elements:**
- Flat horizontal base
- Rising arc (organic, warm curve)
- Vertical intersecting line (structural counterpoint)
- Negative-space window at the intersection (the discovery)

---

## Design Requirements

- **Style:** Simple geometric symbol mark
- **Reference:** Cloudflare logo — clean, minimal, recognizable at 16x16px
- **Type:** Symbol mark only (wordmark is secondary — the word "Milkcrate" sits beside or below the mark in a clean typeface)
- **Color:** Single-color friendly (for favicon, app icon, print, one-color applications)
- **Scale:** Must work at favicon size (16x16px) through large hero graphics
- **Feel:** Warm, human, inviting — not cold, techy, or industrial
- **Theme:** Discovery, opening, reveal — finding something great you wouldn't have found otherwise
- **Not:** A literal crate, a literal record, or any obvious music iconography
- **Versatility:** Use cases include website favicon, app icon, social media avatar, print on bags/cards, storefront badge, small nav logo

---

## What This Direction Avoids

- No literal crate illustration
- No literal record/vinyl icon
- No music notes or turntable imagery
- No milk/beverage imagery
- No overly complex or illustrative mark
- No cold/tech geometric abstraction (the arc keeps it human)

---

## Process Notes

From the brainstorm conversation on 2026-05-25:

- User wanted something beyond a wordmark, but simple like Cloudflare
- Early exploration considered crate forms, record+crate merges, and stacks. The abstract "discovery/reveal" direction resonated most.
- "Rising arc" and "intersection/overlap" were the two strongest sub-directions. A blend of both — an arc crossing a line with negative space at the seam — became the final direction.
- The brand's core is discovery: "Browse Discogs like a record store, not an inventory."
